    How the numbers are worked out

    kWh / day = watts × hours per day ÷ 1000

    kWh / month = kWh per day × (days per week ÷ 7) × 30.44

    cost = kWh × your rate per kWh

    30.44 is the average number of days in a month, so a 7-day-a-week appliance is counted for a full month. For always-on or cycling appliances like a fridge or router, the defaults use an effective wattage — a fridge doesn't pull its full compressor rating around the clock, so counting it at that would badly overstate the cost. Change any number to match your own appliance, and the ranking and totals update instantly.

    Estimates only — not a metered reading or professional energy audit. Figures come from your inputs and typical wattages, and are meant to help you compare and prioritise, not to predict your exact bill. For precise numbers, read your meter or ask a qualified electrician or energy assessor.
